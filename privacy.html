Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how Spectrum Behavioral Solutions collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards your information when you visit our website and use our services.

Last Updated: January 2025

1. Introduction

Spectrum Behavioral Solutions ("we," "us," "our," or "Company") is committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have a positive experience on our website and when using our behavioral therapy services. This Privacy Policy outlines our practices regarding the collection, use, and protection of your personal information.

We comply with all applicable privacy laws and regulations, including the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), where applicable. If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our privacy practices, please contact us using the information provided at the end of this document.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information from you in various ways to provide and improve our services. The types of information we collect include:

Personal Information

When you schedule a consultation or register for our services, we collect personal information such as your name, email address, phone number, date of birth, and address. We may also collect information about your medical history, mental health background, behavioral concerns, and therapeutic goals necessary to provide you with appropriate care and treatment.

Health Information

As a behavioral health provider, we collect detailed health information including your medical history, current medications, previous therapy experiences, behavioral assessments, progress notes, and treatment records. This information is protected under HIPAA and other applicable health privacy laws and is maintained in secure, confidential records.

Website Usage Information

When you visit our website, we automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing behavior, including your IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, pages visited, time spent on pages, and other similar information. This helps us understand how visitors use our website and improve our online services.

Communication Information

We may collect information from any communications you have with us, including emails, phone calls, messages through our website, and feedback or inquiries you submit. This information helps us respond to your requests and improve our customer service.

Payment Information

If you make payments for our services, we collect payment information including credit card details, billing address, and transaction history. Payment processing is handled by secure third-party payment processors, and we do not store complete credit card information on our servers.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for various purposes, including:

  • Providing Behavioral Therapy Services

    To deliver, maintain, and improve our behavioral therapy services, including conducting assessments, developing treatment plans, and tracking your progress toward therapeutic goals.

  • Communication

    To communicate with you about your therapy, appointment scheduling, treatment updates, and any changes to our services or policies.

  • Billing and Payment Processing

    To process payments, send invoices, and manage billing for our services. We may also use this information for financial planning and record-keeping purposes.

  • Website Improvement

    To analyze website usage patterns, understand user preferences, and improve the functionality, content, and user experience of our website.

  • Legal Compliance

    To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes, including HIPAA requirements and court orders when necessary.

  • Research and Quality Improvement

    To conduct research, quality improvement initiatives, and outcome studies that help us provide better behavioral health services (always in compliance with privacy regulations).

4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We are committed to protecting your privacy and do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. However, we may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

Service Providers

We may share information with trusted service providers who assist us in operating our website, conducting our business, and providing services to you. These service providers are contractually obligated to maintain the confidentiality and security of your information and may only use it to perform services on our behalf.

Emergency Situations

We may disclose your information when required by law or when we believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to protect the safety of you or others, prevent fraud, or respond to legal process such as a subpoena or court order.

Consent-Based Sharing

With your explicit written consent, we may share your health information with other healthcare providers, insurance companies, or other parties as you authorize. You may withdraw this consent at any time.

Business Transfers

If Spectrum Behavioral Solutions is involved in a merger, acquisition, bankruptcy, dissolution, reorganization, or similar transaction or proceeding, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will provide notice before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience and collect information about how you interact with our site. Here's what you need to know:

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit our website. They help us remember your preferences, keep you logged in, and track your browsing behavior to improve our services.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • • Essential Cookies: Required for basic website functionality, security, and user authentication.
  • • Performance Cookies: Help us understand how you use our website and identify areas for improvement.
  • • Preference Cookies: Remember your choices and settings to personalize your experience.
  • • Marketing Cookies: Used to track your interactions and deliver targeted content (only with your consent).

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through your browser settings. You can choose to reject cookies or receive notifications when a cookie is being sent. However, please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality and performance of our website.

You can typically manage cookies by going to your browser preferences or settings. For more information about cookies and how to manage them, visit www.allaboutcookies.org.

Third-Party Tracking

We may use third-party analytics services, such as Google Analytics, to track website usage and performance. These third parties may use their own cookies and tracking technologies. We recommend reviewing their privacy policies to understand how they handle your information.

6. Data Security

We take the security of your personal and health information seriously and implement comprehensive safeguards to protect it from unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

  • Encryption

    We use industry-standard SSL/TLS encryption to protect data transmitted between your device and our servers.

  • Access Controls

    We restrict access to personal and health information to authorized personnel only, who are trained in privacy and security protocols.

  • Firewalls and Intrusion Detection

    We employ firewalls and intrusion detection systems to monitor and prevent unauthorized access to our systems.

  • Regular Security Audits

    We conduct regular security assessments and penetration testing to identify and address potential vulnerabilities.

  • Data Retention Policies

    We maintain information only as long as necessary for the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy or as required by law.

While we implement robust security measures, no system is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information. If you believe your information has been compromised, please contact us immediately.

7. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location and applicable laws, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information:

Right to Access

You have the right to request access to the personal and health information we hold about you. We will provide you with a copy of your information within the timeframe required by applicable law.

Right to Correction

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information. We will update your records promptly upon receiving a valid request.

Right to Deletion

In certain circumstances, you may request deletion of your information. However, we may retain information as required by law or for legitimate business purposes, including maintaining treatment records.

Right to Opt-Out

You may opt out of receiving marketing communications from us at any time by following the unsubscribe instructions in our emails or by contacting us directly.

Right to Data Portability

You may request a copy of your information in a portable format that can be transferred to another organization.

8. Children's Privacy

Our website and services are not intended for children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete such information and terminate the child's use of our services. If you believe we have collected information from a child under 13, please contact us immediately. For children 13 and older, we provide behavioral therapy services with appropriate parental consent and involvement as required by law and clinical best practices.

9. Third-Party Links and Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites and services that are not operated by Spectrum Behavioral Solutions. This Privacy Policy does not apply to third-party websites, and we are not responsible for their privacy practices. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party websites before providing your personal information. Your use of third-party services is at your own risk and subject to their terms and conditions.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the updated Privacy Policy on our website and updating the "Last Updated" date at the top of this document.

Your continued use of our website or services after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy. We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.
